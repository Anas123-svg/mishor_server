Controls

Risk Control Measures
the roof edge Full edge protection (top rail, toe board and intermediate protection) is required whenever the work requires access within 2m of the roof perimeter. Edge protection shall be erected by competent persons around the building periphery/working area before work commencement. All operatives are to be trained in safe systems of roof work, safe use of equipment, harness and fall prevention equipment. Where work on a larger roof involves no operatives closer than 2m to the edge of the roof, a demarcation area can be used in place of edge protection, strictly controlled with no access outside the area during work or when accessing the work area. Working area and routes to it to be marked out with continuous physical barriers and strictly supervised. Netting is to be installed where practicable below all work areas. Operatives installing equipment such as netting, scaffolding, roof ladders etc. will be trained in the correct installation methods. All debris is to be cleared out of netting as soon as is practicable.
through the roof Where the roof does not provide a safe working platform a temporary safe working platform to be installed or mobile access such as MEWP is used as a work platform. Gaps, holes or fragile materials are to be covered with a material which is fixed in position and sturdy enough to take the weight of a person or edge protection fixed around the gap to prevent access. Operatives are to be fitted with work restraint safety harnesses with a sufficiently short lanyard preventing reaching a position from which they could fall and attached to a secure anchorage /running line system. Safety nets are to be installed as close as possible beneath the roof surface, securely attached and will withstand a person falling onto them, installed and maintained by competent personnel.
Falling items Injuries to others from tools or materials Roof area to be kept clear and tidy, free from build-up of materials. Materials stored away from access routes and work areas. Toe board and brick guard to be incorporated in edge protection where there is a risk of objects being kicked off the edge of the platform. Rubbish chutes are to be installed where appropriate or materials are to be lowered in containers – throwing materials from height is strictly prohibited at all times. Access underneath or adjacent to the roof work area will be prohibited. Where access is required debris netting or covered walkways will be used to minimise risk.
Weather Increased risk due to weather conditions Weather conditions and forecast checked before starting roof work. Work is prohibited if ice is present on the roof, or in windy conditions or heavy rain. Weather is monitored by the supervisor and work is stopped if conditions become unsafe. Excessive exposure to sunlight is prevented through clothing and sun creams.
Falls during access to roof level Access to the roof via a scaffold system erected by a competent contractor. Stairway design preferred - minimum requirement fixed ladder. Carry out suitable inspection of scaffold weekly or after substantial alteration or adverse weather conditions, recorded inspections in the site register. Ensure access routes on the roof are sufficient and any fragile material is avoided unless suitable platforms and sheeting are positioned spanning roof supports. Access points secured with heras-type fencing. Very short duration work under 2 hours will be controlled through a safe means of access to the roof via fixed ladder or mobile access equipment, and a safe method of work with harness, and roof ladder on sloping roofs.
Slips and trips hazards Falls Work area and access route to be free of trip hazards. Access areas and work areas are to be kept clear of debris and surplus materials. Good housekeeping and waste removal arrangements are in place.
Fragile materials Falls through fragile roof materials Roof materials will be checked by a competent person before work starts. Fragile roofs shall be clearly signed and permit systems shall authorise and control access. Fragile materials are to be covered with a material which is fixed in position and sturdy enough to take the weight of a person or edge protection fixed around the material to prevent access. Where access is required netting systems or airbags (if lower level) shall be employed. Netting systems shall be erected by competent persons accompanied by a compliant ‘sign off’ certificate. All existing roof lights shall be securely covered and protected before work commences. All work at height to be off non-fragile platforms spanning purlins and fixed roofing panels. No working on fragile materials. Liner panels, unfixed or partially fixed materials are considered fragile unless it has been conclusively confirmed that they are not. Where work is to be carried out in areas where staging is impracticable or short duration, harnesses with fall restraint in combination with safety netting may be used.
Hazardous substances Skin exposure to mortar Ensure welfare and hygiene facilities are in place to avoid dermatitis. Operatives trained in the risks from contact with dust and mortar. Work area well ventilated to prevent the build-up of cement dust. Keep skin clean. Carry out a skin monitoring programme. Gloves and full-length sleeves are to be worn to minimise skin exposure. If clothing becomes heavily contaminated with wet mortar mix, operative to change to reduce skin exposure.
Pitched roof Increased risk when working on pitched roofs No work directly off slates or tiles. Safe footing is to be provided through the use of roof ladders and proprietary staging to enable safe passage across a roof securely fixed using a ridge hook placed over the ridge, bearing on the opposite roof. Eaves-level edge protection to be installed for long-duration work. Edge protection at gable ends if work within 2m.
Work near gas flues Damage or obstruction The roof is assessed for gas flues and extraction vents before work commences. An exclusion zone is established to prevent damage or obstruction of flue. Where work is close to gas flues or extraction vents, ensure the work does not affect the flue. Take care to ensure the flue is not damaged, dislodged or blocked during the work. Any accidental damage must be immediately reported and assessed by a competent person.
Manual handling Injury from handling of materials and equipment All staff are trained in manual handling techniques. Ensure the workplace is kept tidy and walkways free from trip hazards. Loads are supplied so they can be broken down into manageable loads for lifting and carrying. Use mechanical means to handle large, heavy and awkward loads. Hoists are used where necessary for lifting large quantities to the roof level.
Hand tools Injury from the use of hand tools Correct use of tools per manufacturer’s recommendations at all times and specific risk assessments for hand tools/equipment where risks are significant. Gloves and eye protection are to be worn during any cutting operations or use of power tools. Only the correct accessories will be used with the equipment and must be suitable for the material on which it will be used. All site supplies will be 110 volts. Operatives must check the condition of all electric power tools before use and report any faults. PAT testing is required for all portable electrical equipment.
Risk to others or unauthorised use Unauthorised access is prevented by blocking off or removing access ladders or scaffolding and displaying warning signs. Permit-to-work system implemented for access. Access points are to be checked at the end of each working day to ensure that access to the roof level by children or trespass has been prevented.
Members of the public Increased risk to the public from falling items An exclusion zone is established at ground level where overhead work is carried out. Where an exclusion zone cannot be established, safety netting or other barriers are installed above any areas with public access to protect from debris.
